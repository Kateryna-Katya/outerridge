The World
Behind The Altar

Nobody really knows how long these stone guardians have been standing here. Could be centuries, could be longer.
Whoever built these things was no amateur. Makes our modern tech look like toys, honestly. Whoever built this stuff really knew what they were doing. Pretty straightforward job: keep watch and make sure nothing nasty crawls out of demon-land.

In X-Demon, you step into that legacy. The altar is awake again.
Something on the other side is pushing back, and only a handful of symbols, relics, and defensive mechanisms remain intact. This isn't just about killing demons - you're literally the only thing stopping the world from ending.

Wild Attack Mechanic

Wild Attack — When Ancient Power Awakens

Along the top row of the altar, special Wild Attack symbols sometimes carve through the darkness.

  • When one shows up over a Demon symbol, the stone just splits open and this crazy energy pours out. The demon totally loses it and just goes nuts, destroying everything in sight.

  • Wilds do what you'd expect - they'll stand in for pretty much anything, except for those ritual marks (those are special).

Ritual Mark Symbol

Ritual Marks — The Ancient Mechanism Reacts

Hidden within the altar are Ritual Mark symbols.
Finding multiple marks during a single sequence activates deeper stages of the ritual — then you get thrown into this special fight where it's not just about staying alive anymore - now you gotta actually clean house in demon territory.
Get enough marks together and all hell breaks loose - literally. The portal starts gaping wider.

The Demon Encounter Round

Demon Encounter — Confront the Underworld

In the encounter rounds, each demon has this little counter showing how much fight it's got left in it.
When a Wild Attack channels energy into a Demon during this phase, part of its essence burns away. Bit by bit, symbol by symbol, the Demons weaken.
Once a Demon’s tally reaches zero, it collapses into a Golden Wild, keeping its empowered form for the remainder of the encounter.
And that's when you know you're getting somewhere - every little victory actually matters.
